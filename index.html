A Non-Traditional Path

The detour was
the education

Columbia, MD — navigating the road ahead.

"I don't view my journey as time lost.
I view it as the foundation that shaped how I approach challenges today."

My path into technology wasn't linear.

I arrived to the United States about a decade and a half ago. I started out with the dream to be a doctor but life had other plans. While many of my peers followed a traditional college-to-career trajectory, I spent several years navigating immigration challenges, helping my family, and learning firsthand how quickly life can change. Those experiences delayed my graduation — but they also developed qualities that can't be taught in a classroom: resilience, patience, adaptability, and perspective.

When I returned to complete my degree after about a three years detour, I approached it differently. I became deeply interested in data, cloud technologies, and AI systems — not just as technical tools, but as ways to solve real-world problems for real people. Since graduating, I've focused on building projects that combine data engineering, cloud infrastructure, and AI to create practical business solutions.

Staying curious, keep building, and continue moving with the right attitude has become my north star.
